Visiting Batticaloa Lagoon

The wide waves lagoon referred to as Batticaloa Lagoon separates Batticaloa from the Indian Ocean. The lagoon is well-known for its "performing fish," as it is claimed that, during full moons, posed a threat marine life produces audible but weak sounds that come out from its waters near Kallady Bridge. These sounds mimic those of a played guitar. Enjoy sailing and floating among the rice and coconut fields, Batticaloa Fort, fishing villages, and stunning sunset as you go around the lagoon.
